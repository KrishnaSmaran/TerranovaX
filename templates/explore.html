📚 Disaster Awareness Hub

Know your risks. Be prepared. Stay safe.

🌍

Earthquakes

Earthquakes occur when tectonic plates shift. India sits on the Indian Plate bordered by the Eurasian and Indo-Australian plates, making many regions highly seismic.

📊 India has 59% of its land in earthquake-prone zones.
🌊

Floods

Floods are the most common disaster in India, affecting millions annually. Caused by heavy monsoon rains, dam failures, and poor drainage in urban areas.

📊 India loses ₹4,800 crore annually to flood damage.
🌪️

Cyclones

The Bay of Bengal and Arabian Sea generate some of the world's most intense cyclones. India's eastern coast is highly vulnerable.

📊 Bay of Bengal produces 7% of global tropical cyclones.
🔥

Heatwaves

A heatwave is declared when temperature is 4.5°C above normal for 2+ days. India's northwest and central regions face extreme heat events every summer.

📊 Heatwaves killed 6,000+ people in India between 2015–2022.
🌫️

Air Quality

Air pollution causes more deaths than floods and cyclones combined in India. PM2.5 particles penetrate deep into lungs causing serious disease.

📊 India has 22 of the world's 30 most polluted cities.
🌊

Tsunamis

Triggered by underwater earthquakes, tsunamis can travel at 800km/h. India's Andaman & Nicobar Islands are at significant risk.

📊 The 2004 Indian Ocean Tsunami affected 14 countries.
🏔️

Landslides

Heavy monsoon rains trigger landslides in hilly states like Kerala, Uttarakhand, and Himachal Pradesh.

📊 India accounts for 30% of global landslide fatalities.
🌋

Volcanoes

India's only active volcano is Barren Island in the Andaman Sea. Global volcanic activity can affect worldwide climate patterns significantly.

📊 There are ~1,500 potentially active volcanoes worldwide.
